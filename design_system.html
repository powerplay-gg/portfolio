Overview

YouDo Business is the biggest platform in Russia for engaging with freelancers. The company offers a range of services to facilitate the formation of the requisite documentation, the identification of contractors, and the payment of taxes, catering to over 20,000 companies on a monthly basis. Furthermore, in excess of $50 million is remitted to 3.5 million performers each month.

Role

As a sole senior product designer I led developing and improving the design system almost from scratch. This involved creating components using Figma, driving all process to deliver it into production, budget approvalling and bringing it to production.

Business goals

Accelerating the development of new features

Design systems aid in the minimization of both design and development time and expenses, thereby allowing teams to concentrate on more advanced design responsibilities.

Improving UX and brand awareness

Design systems serve to guarantee that all design elements within a product or organization maintain consistency, establishing a cohesive and recognizable brand identity, and simultaneously elevating user experience.

Business scaling

As an organization or product grows, design systems can aid in the preservation of consistent and maintainable design elements, even in the face of an escalating level of complexity.

Working process

Research and analysis

Upon my arrival at YouDo, my primary assignment was to tackle the design system. The product had already outgrown the MVP stage and was undergoing active development. However, the design system was practically nonexistent, and this impeded the swift development of new features. Following a thorough examination of the existing state of affairs, I identified a series of crucial issues.

  • there was no common component base; either old or neighboring components were used, or not used at all
  • consequently, there was a lack of a distinct component structure, rendering it unfeasible to locate the desired element
  • there was no font library in Figma
  • colors were used excessively, pulled from a neighboring product, and applied randomly, resulting in an excessively bright and chaotic appearance

There are some examples of previouse condition of design system

I converted the issues into tasks and then proceeded to break them down. Here's a summary of the outcome:

Independent work

  • collecting a single component base in Figma, using all its power (variants, auto-layouts, etc.)
  • developing own font styles and color palette exclusively for b2b
  • injecting it all into layouts, establishing dependencies
  • writing guidelines how to use it all

Team work

  • presentation of the design system to budget stakeholders, explanation of its advantages, and coordination of the budget for its development
  • presentation and communication with the development for the implementation of a common component base and tokens
  • control, monitoring and assistance in implementing the design system in code

Let's work!

First of all, I began working independently on the component base and made daily improvements to it. I also designed new components using Figma in conjunction with product development, since no dedicated time was allocated for system design. After approximately three months of such efforts, I was able to compile nearly all of the design elements into components, standardized the basic components, reduced the number of colors used in the product from 57 to 22, consolidated the components, fonts, and colors into a single Figma file, and established a clear structure to eliminate confusion. Some examples of the design components are provided below.

The next step was the most challenging aspect - presenting the work and coordinating the budget for the development of the code. By enlisting the assistance of some developers, I was able to persuade the business of the necessity for such a large-scale project. However, since the company was undergoing a challenging phase during that period, there were no available resources to address it. Consequently, we postponed it until the end of the year, but ultimately resumed the project in November as previously agreed. Once the budget was allocated, the developers began coding components and tokens with great enthusiasm. I remained in constant communication, aided them, and addressed their inquiries.

Getting help from some members of my team was the key factor in completing this difficult project. When you work for a big company with many people involved, you have to navigate a lot of politics to succeed and do great work.

Result

In collaboration with the development team, we were able to synchronize and code the majority of the components. Consequently, we have accomplished the primary business goals:

  • the acceleration of feature development surpassed 30%
  • the quantity of design questions from the development team has decreased, thereby releasing my resources for product-related tasks
  • throughout the development process, I delved deeper into the user experience aspect and collaborated with the development team to rectify numerous minor issues such as validation and these efforts resulted in an enhanced user experience
  • as a result of reduced development costs, we were able to identify and pursue more substantial features
  • and it just became a little more pleasant for the team to live 🙂
