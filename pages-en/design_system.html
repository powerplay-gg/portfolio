YouDo Business, 2022

YouDo Business Design System

Building a complete product design system

About the company

YouDo Business is a leading Russian platform for working with self-employed contractors. The company provides services for document management, contractor operations, and tax payments, serving over 20,000 companies monthly. More than 3.5 billion rubles are paid out each month to 3.5 million contractors.

My role

As the only Senior Product Designer, I led design system development and evolution almost from scratch, including component creation in Figma, rollout coordination, implementation management, and budget alignment.

Business goals

A design system reduces design and development time, allowing teams to focus on higher-value tasks. It improves UI consistency, strengthens brand identity, and improves user experience. As product complexity grows, a design system also keeps design operations manageable and scalable.

Research & analysis

When I joined YouDo, my first priority was solving design-system related issues. The product had already moved beyond MVP and was growing actively, but the absence of a mature design system made feature delivery slower. After analyzing the current state, I identified several critical problems.

  • no shared component base; teams used outdated components, components from adjacent products, or no components at all
  • no clear component structure, making discovery and reuse difficult
  • no proper typography library in Figma
  • too many colors were used inconsistently, creating a visually noisy and chaotic interface

Here is a short summary of what we achieved after converting those problems into concrete tasks and decomposing them.

Individual work:

  • built a component base in Figma using advanced capabilities (variants, auto-layout, etc.)
  • created custom typography and color libraries
  • restructured layouts and established dependencies
  • wrote guidelines

Team effort:

  • presented the design system to stakeholders, explained value, and aligned the rollout plan
  • collaborated with engineers to implement a shared component and token base
  • monitored implementation and supported the team during rollout

Execution

I started by improving the component base daily. New Figma components were built in parallel with product feature delivery, because there was no dedicated design-system time allocation. After roughly three months, we had most elements converted into components, standardized the core layer, reduced the product color palette from 57 to 22 colors, and consolidated components, typography, and colors in a single structured file. This removed ambiguity and made the system usable.

The next challenge was securing budget and implementation buy-in. With support from several engineers, I was able to demonstrate the need for this large-scale initiative. Because the company was in a difficult period, there were no immediate resources, so we moved implementation to later in the year and resumed in November, as agreed. The engineering team started implementing components and tokens with high motivation. I stayed closely involved, providing support and answering questions.

Support from colleagues was the key factor that moved this project forward. In a large company with many stakeholders, you must navigate organizational and political constraints to deliver meaningful improvements.

Result

Together with the engineering team, we synchronized and implemented most of the core components.

  • new feature delivery accelerated by more than 30%
  • design-related questions from engineering dropped, freeing my time for product work
  • while rolling out the system, I also improved UX and fixed many smaller issues, including validation-related problems
  • faster delivery allowed us to work on more complex features
  • overall team workflow became much easier

This project gave me strong practical experience in system-level design and implementation collaboration, and deepened my product UX expertise.

Later, the team kept scaling the system and used it as a foundation for product development and quality growth.
