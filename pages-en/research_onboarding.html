Onboarding Research

Defining the best ways to introduce users to the product

Problem

We do not explain how the product and interface work. Users are either left on their own or forced to contact support. If we scale traffic to the current version, support may not handle the load.

Problem exploration

User autonomy can be improved in many ways: from basic UI tips to interactive product tours and a full help center. For MVP, we chose a lower-cost option: contextual in-product hints. They should be limited in number to avoid annoying users, but it was unclear where exactly users need help and what they do not understand.

Hypothesis

We need to place contextual hints across the interface so users can quickly understand how to use the product on their own.

Research

At project kickoff, I analyzed the current product state. During the global redesign, responsive behavior had been addressed as a residual task. Redesigning without proper mobile adaptation is risky, but we did not have enough resources to treat mobile with the same depth as desktop. The challenge was increased by my limited mobile-device design experience at that moment, so producing high-quality mobile UI immediately was difficult. Over time, while working on each travel service, I gradually improved responsive layouts, and each service ended up with its own slightly customized responsive version.

Results

  • Users rarely make mistakes in basic actions and generally do not need explanations about where controls are located. Even older users usually figure it out through exploration.
  • Most problems appear later in the flow, from adding contractors to completing payouts.
  • The most common issues are caused by lack of UI responsiveness, for example when the interface does not validate errors clearly.

Conclusion

It looks like detailed contextual hints are not necessary. We can use lightweight contextual popups with sliders that explain, at a high level, what happens in each part of the product. To increase user autonomy, we should fix error-handling issues in the interface.
