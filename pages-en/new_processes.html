Loginet, 2021

New Design Team Processes

As a manager, I improved existing workflows and introduced new ones in the design team

Problem

In May 2021, my manager left and I had to work alone. For two months, we could not find either a new manager or a mid-level designer. It became clear we could not continue like this, so I initiated hiring a junior designer. After hiring two juniors, I realized we needed to introduce new team processes.

Solution

Sprint-based workflow

Previously we worked without structured tasks or sprints, and that was manageable for a while. After onboarding two new designers and increasing the number of projects, I organized the design team around weekly sprints with task tracking. At the start of each week, we synced with the Product Owner to review completed work, define priorities, and agree on the weekly backlog.

Weekly retrospectives

To improve adaptation and growth, I started weekly 1:1 meetings with each designer. We reviewed the past week, discussed completed tasks and blockers, identified growth opportunities, and defined the plan for the next week.

Design debt management

After two years of product growth, many issues had accumulated in implementation quality. Given rising product complexity and delivery intensity, this could have led to either design quality decline or longer development cycles. I created a prioritized list of issues: inaccuracies in layouts, outdated interaction patterns, usability problems, and more. These were converted into tasks and distributed across sprints.

Building a full design system

As the product grew, design could not scale without clearly documented rules. We had defined some principles with my previous manager, but there was no single knowledge base describing components and interaction patterns. Guidelines helped new team members ramp up faster, improved cross-team collaboration, and reduced the number of requests to designers. By November 2021, we had already presented the guideline structure and documented several core elements to the team.
