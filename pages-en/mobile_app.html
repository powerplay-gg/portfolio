Aeroclub, 2024

LINK Mobile App

Transition from a responsive web version to a native mobile app

About the company

Aeroclub Group is a leader in the Russian business travel market, developing and delivering IT solutions and services for businesses, and acting as an exclusive partner of the global BCD Travel network.

My role

As a Senior Product Designer, I was responsible for designing the mobile application, defining key patterns and navigation, and adapting the existing web product design to mobile interaction patterns. I worked closely with the product manager and mobile developer.

Business goals

A key reason for building the app was brand positioning: all major players in the market already had mobile apps that provide a more convenient and modern customer experience. Mobile apps are typically faster than responsive websites, support push notifications, and can work offline, which is especially important in travel.

Research & analysis

At the start of the project, I analyzed the current state of the product. During a large-scale redesign, mobile adaptation had been treated as a secondary task. It would have been odd to redesign without mobile support, but we did not have desktop-level resources. The situation was also affected by my limited mobile design experience at that time, so producing high-quality mobile interfaces immediately was difficult. While working on each travel service, I incrementally improved responsive layouts, which led to each service ending up with a slightly different mobile version.

We also needed to rethink product navigation, since its foundation had been created long ago and, like the responsive design, was not suitable enough for a modern mobile application.

Special attention was required for mobile-specific features that were missing in the current version: modern authentication, app-level settings (for example, notifications and biometric login), and more.

In summary: we needed clear and intuitive navigation designed for a native mobile experience, consistent and up-to-date design across all services, and dedicated mobile screens (such as biometric authentication).

After the initial navigation exploration, it became clear that the original solution did not work as expected. I had a familiar screen-transition pattern in mind, but after validating it with the mobile developer, we found this logic would not work in the app. That was when I learned more about mini-app principles and realized we needed to fully rethink the approach.

After iteration, I prepared an updated navigation scheme and aligned it with the mobile developer. Then I presented it to the product manager. The adaptation took around two weeks, and we approved the changes.

At the final stage, we partially rebuilt the design with a focus on modal windows. This helped keep the app lightweight and avoid unnecessary complexity. As a result, the overall navigation logic changed as well. I redesigned the menu, transitions, and interactions between screens, then presented the solution to stakeholders. The concept was approved.

Once the navigation work was complete, I moved on to redesigning authentication. The goal was to create a modern and intuitive sign-in flow for mobile: biometric access, session persistence, and fast re-authentication.

The final and longest phase was updating service layouts. We had to rebuild interfaces for the new navigation, adapt existing screens, and bring visual and UX quality to a single consistent standard.

Result

Unfortunately, I left the company before implementation. Before I departed, we had to quickly align all services to a unified mobile design that met current standards. This took about three weeks of intensive work.

This project gave me valuable hands-on experience in mobile design for iOS/Android environments. I learned a lot about how mobile apps work under the hood and applied mobile interaction patterns in practice.

Later I learned the team successfully continued the work: the project moved confidently toward app release and gradual migration from the legacy web interface to a format much closer to a native mobile experience.
